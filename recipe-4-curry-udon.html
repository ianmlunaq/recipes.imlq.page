Curry Udon üçú

Curry udon is a Japanese dish that combines the comfort of two favorites: udon noodles and Japanese curry. It features thick, chewy udon noodles served in a rich, flavorful curry broth. The curry itself can be made from scratch with a dashi base or use pre-made roux blocks for a quicker version. This hearty noodle soup is known for its satisfying and warming taste, perfect for a quick weeknight meal or a cozy lunch. You might also find variations with additional toppings like sliced pork, tempura vegetables, or even a soft-boiled egg.

Amount to make:

Ingredients:

  • 0.5 yellow onions
  • 3 green onions
  • 3 Japanese curry roux blocks (e.g., Golden Curry, Vermont Curry)
  • 1 carrot
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 14 oz pre-cooked udon noodles

Procedure:

  1. Thinly slice onion and green onions. Set green onions aside for garnish.
  2. Cut carrot in half length-wise, then thinly slice to get little, half-moon carrot slices.
  3. In a deep pan or shallow pot, saut√© onion and carrot in a Tbsp, or so, of oil on medium heat until golden.
  4. Slice garlic and throw into pan along with the roux blocks and as much water as curry package recommends. Cover and let rest until curry blocks have melted.
  5. Add udon and gently wiggle them around to soften and loosen them up.
  6. Serve topped with green onions.
