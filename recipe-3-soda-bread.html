Soda bread is a dense and crumbly quick bread, traditionally from Ireland. Unlike most breads, it skips the yeast and uses baking soda for leavening. This creates a distinct texture with a slightly tangy flavor. The basic recipe uses just flour, milk, baking soda, and salt, but variations can include caraway seeds, raisins, or even a touch of sweetness. It's a hearty bread perfect for toasting and enjoying with butter or a simple stew.

Amount to make:

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1.5 tsp baking POWDER
  • 1.25 cup milk
  • 1 large egg

Procedure:

  1. PREHEAT oven to 425¬∞F.
  2. In a bowl, combine dry ingredients first, then add wet ingredients and combine.
  3. Place dough on a baking tray lined with well-greased aluminium foil.
  4. With wet hands, shape dough into a round-ish loaf, sprinkle with flour, score a cross on top, and cover with a metal bowl.
  5. Bake at 425¬∞F for 30 minutes covered and another 30 minutes uncovered.
